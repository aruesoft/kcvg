Overview

FWD (Front Wheel Drive)
FWD (Front Wheel Drive) is the most popular configuration for personal vehicles. In FWD vehicles, the engine in the front drives the front wheels only, distributing 100% of the power to front wheels.
RWD (Rear Wheel Drive)
In RWD (Rear Wheel Drive) vehicles, the engine in the front drives the rear wheels only. The power is distributed 100% to rear wheels through the propeller shaft.

4WD (Four Wheel Drive) provides power to all wheels. Unlike FWD, power is distributed to the rear wheels through transfer, propeller shaft and rear differential. The power distribution ratio between the front and rear wheels is controlled automatically by 4WD ECU by analyzing the current road and driving conditions.

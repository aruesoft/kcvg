Valves are used to open and close the intake and exhaust ports, in addition to controlling the timing and quantity of the intake and exhaust air.

Valve operation is controlled by rotation of the camshaft. There are two types of valve operation, Hydraulic Lash Adjustment (HLA) and Mechanical Lash Adjustment (MLA).

Hydraulic Lash Adjustment (HLA)
HLA adjusts itself to maintain zero valve clearance by pushing the cam follower to the camshaft through hydraulic pressure. It is designed to compensate the thermal expansion by operating with zero clearance, eliminating the need for periodic adjustment of valve clearance.
Mechanical Lash Adjustment (MLA)
MLA has a small clearance between the camshaft and the MLA tappet. This small space is for thermal expansion, so the periodic adjustment of valve clearance is necessary.
Symptom
  1. Engine misfire
  2. Valve noise
  3. Excessive engine oil consumption
  4. Lack of engine power
  5. Engine hesitation

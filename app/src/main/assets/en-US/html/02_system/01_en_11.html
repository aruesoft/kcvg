Engine Oil

  • Engine Start-up
  • Engine Running
Engine oil is a lubricant used in internal combustion engines. Engine oil reduces the wear or friction of moving components, cools the engine by carrying heat away from moving components, improves sealing, and removes dirt inside the engine.

Oil viscosity is the most important property for maintaining a lubricating film within an engine.

Oil viscosity is a measure of the oil’s resistance to flow. It affects both the wear rate and fuel efficiency of an engine. Viscosity determines how easily the oil is flowing. The lower the viscosity, the easier circulation will be in an engine. The viscosity must be high enough to maintain a lubricating film, but also low enough that oil can flow around the engine.

For the best performance, use the recommended oil grade in your owner’s manual. Engine oil should be replaced as regularly as described in the maintenance table in your owner’s manual.

Symptom
  1. Engine may be stuck because the oil viscosity is too low
  2. Fuel efficiency may be decreased
  3. A noise may be heard

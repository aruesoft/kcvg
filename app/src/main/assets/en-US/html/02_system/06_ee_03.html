Ignition Coil / Spark Plug

Ignition Coil
Spark Plug
The ignition coil is a type of transformer that produces the high voltage needed to ignite the gasoline engine.
The ignition coil is also used to raise the voltage of the 12V battery to the firing voltage, exceeding 20,000 V.
The spark plug is a device that uses a spark to ignite the compressed fuel / air mixture by delivering an electric current from the ignition system to the combustion chamber of a spark-ignition engine.
This ignites the spark at the right time and burns the compressed fuel-air mixture in the combustion chamber.
The engine is thus operated.
Symptom
  1. Malfunction of starting system (the car engine cranks, but doesn’t start)
  2. Engine hesitation, poor acceleration and lack of engine output

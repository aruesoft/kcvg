DPF (Diesel Particulate Filter)

The DPF is a device used to reduce particulate matter emitted in the exhaust gas.
This device is used to collect and burn particluate matter from the exhaust gas produced by a diesel engine.
The DPF collects particulate matter in the same manner as the filter, and burns it off periodically once the amount of collected particluate matter reaches a certain level.
The fuel used for burning the particulate matter is not injected directly into the DPF. It is injected into the exhaust stroke using a cylinder. This process allows the fuel to flow naturally into the DPF.
The fuel reacts with the oxygen remaining in the exhaust gas, thereby burning the particulate matter (which are mainly composed of carbon particles).
Symptom
  1. Malfuction of the DPF system (DPF warning lamp illuminates)
  2. Lack of engine output due to blockage in the DPF system

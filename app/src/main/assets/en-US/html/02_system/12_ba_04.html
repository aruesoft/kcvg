Tires provide cushioning from bumps and irregularities in road surfaces.
Tires also provide traction for driving, steering, and stopping, which is vital for safety and vehicle performance.
For safe driving, optimal tire pressure should be maintained.
Customers should check tire pressure and tread wear periodically.
Possible cause according to tire wear type
Center

  1. High pressure of tire
  2. Excessive toe on drive wheels
  3. Rapid acceleration habit
Both sides

  1. Low pressure of tire
  2. Damaged suspension components
  3. Excessive abrupt turn driving
One side

  1. Incorrect toe adjustment
    (Out of specification)
  2. Incorrect camber adjustment
  3. Damaged suspension components
Partial

  1. Failure of brake system
  2. Excessive rapid braking
Edge

  1. Damaged tie rod end
  2. Damaged knuckle
  3. Incorrect toe adjustment
    (Out of specification)
Diagonal

  1. Excessive toe on non-drive wheels
Symptom
  1. Vibration due to low tire pressure
  2. TPMS warning light
  3. Abnormal tire wear
  4. Poor braking performance
